Overview

AverageHuman is a collection of browser-based cognitive benchmarks. All tests run entirely in your browser — no data is ever transmitted to a server, stored in a database, or shared with any third party.

This page explains in plain language what information the site touches, where it stays, and how you can remove it at any time.

What is stored on your device

To show you your personal best and attempt history on each test, AverageHuman writes a small record to your browser's localStorage — a storage area that lives only on your device and is never sent anywhere.

For each test you complete, the following is saved locally:

  • Your score or performance metric for that attempt
  • Your personal best across all attempts
  • Total number of attempts
  • A short history of recent results (used to display trends)

This data has no identifying information attached. It is a plain number — indistinguishable from any other user's result if it were ever seen by anyone, which it is not.

What we do not collect
  • Your name, email address, or any account information
  • Your IP address or approximate location
  • Device identifiers, browser fingerprints, or hardware specs
  • Behavioral tracking or click/scroll heatmaps
  • Advertising identifiers of any kind
  • Any information about who you are
localStorage in detail

localStorage is a standard browser feature. Data written there stays on your computer, in your browser profile. It does not expire on its own, it is not a cookie, and it is not shared between different browsers or devices.

The keys AverageHuman writes follow the pattern ah_<test-name> (for example, ah_tapping). You can inspect, modify, or delete them at any time through your browser's developer tools.

To clear all AverageHuman data: open DevTools → Application → Local Storage → select this site → delete the relevant keys, or simply clear your browser's site data for this domain.

Third-party services

AverageHuman loads fonts from Google Fonts via a standard stylesheet request. This means your browser makes a network request to Google's servers to download the font files. Google may log this request as they would any web request to their CDN. Refer to Google's Privacy Policy for details on how they handle font requests.

No other third-party scripts, SDKs, analytics tools, or ad networks are loaded or embedded anywhere on this site.

Children

AverageHuman does not collect personal data from anyone, including children. Because no personal information is collected at all, there are no special restrictions based on age.

Changes to this policy

If the privacy practices of this site ever change in a meaningful way — for example, if server-side features were added — this page will be updated and the "Last updated" date at the top will reflect that change. The core principle will remain: collect the minimum necessary, keep it local, share nothing.

Questions about this policy? The project is open and transparent by design. You can inspect every line of code that runs on this site directly in your browser's developer tools — no build step, no obfuscation.
